\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
The {\ttfamily \mbox{\hyperlink{interfacejavazoom_1_1jl_1_1player_1_1_audio_device}{Audio\+Device}}} interface provides an abstraction for a device capable of sounding audio samples. Samples are written to the device wia the \mbox{\hyperlink{interfacejavazoom_1_1jl_1_1player_1_1_audio_device_a5e3f4e6b862a89b2bb948222d2ee6a74}{write()}} method. The device assumes that these samples are signed 16-\/bit samples taken at the output frequency of the decoder. If the decoder outputs more than one channel, the samples for each channel are assumed to appear consecutively, with the lower numbered channels preceeding higher-\/numbered channels. E.\+g. if there are two channels, the samples will appear in this order\+: 
\begin{DoxyPre}{\ttfamily }\end{DoxyPre}



\begin{DoxyPre}{\ttfamily     l0, r0, l1, r1, l2, r2...}\end{DoxyPre}



\begin{DoxyPre}{\ttfamily where 
 l{\itshape x} indicates the {\itshape x}th sample on channel 0
 r{\itshape x} indicates the {\itshape x}th sample on channel 1
}\end{DoxyPre}
